Eminem

The undisputed rap GOAT

Here's a time line of Eminem's life:

  • 1972 - Born in St. Joseph, Missouri, U.S.
  • 1996 - Release of his debut album Infinite.

    Formed D12 with Proof, Bizarre, Mr. Porter, Kuniva and Swifty McVay.
  • 1997 - Released Slim Shady EP and The Underground EP (with D12).
  • 1998 - Signed with Dr. Dre's Aftermath Entertainment.

    Formed Bad Meets Evil with Royce da 5'9".
  • 1999 - Achieved mainstream popularity with The Slim Shady LP.

    Founded Shady Records with his manager Paul Rosenberg.
  • 2000 - Released The Marshall Mathers LP, a worldwide success nominated for the Grammy Award for Album of the Year. The album sold 1.78 million copies in its first week, which made it among the fastest-selling studio albums in the United States.
  • 2001 - Released the album Devil's Night with D12.

    Made a cameo appearance in the film The Wash.
  • 2002 - Released The Eminem Show, another worldwide success nominated for the Grammy Award for Album of the Year. Debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and stood there for six non-consecutive weeks.

    Made his debut in the film industry with the musical drama film 8 Mile, playing a dramatized version of himself, and his track "Lose Yourself" from its soundtrack won the Academy Award for Best Original Song, making him the first hip hop artist ever to win the award.
  • 2004 - Released Encore and D12 World (with D12).

    Founded Shade 45, a hip-hop station on Sirius XM Radio channel 45 and Dish Network channel 6045.
  • 2005 - Released Curtain Call: The Hits, his first greatest hits album.

    Went on hiatus, largely due to a prescription drug addiction.
  • 2009 - Returned to the music industry with the release of Relapse.

    Made a cameo appearance in the film Funny People.
  • 2010 - Released Recovery, the bestselling album worldwide of 2010, making it Eminem's second album, after The Eminem Show in 2002, to be the best-selling album of the year worldwide.

    Made a cameo appearance in the television series Entourage.
  • 2011 - Released Hell: The Sequel (with Royce da 5'9").
  • 2013 - Released the US number one album The Marshall Mathers LP 2.
  • 2014 - Released Shady XV (with Shady Records).

    Made a cameo appearance in the film The Interview.
  • 2015 - Released Southpaw (Music from and Inspired by the Motion Picture) with various artists.

    Made a cameo appearance in the film The Interview.
  • 2017 - Released another US number one album, Revival.
  • 2018 - Released Kamikaze. US number one album, again.
  • 2020 - Released Music to Be Murdered By. Yes, US number one album.
  • 2022 - Released Curtain Call 2. A sequel to Curtain Call: The Hits (2005).

    Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

In his own way, he is the best lyricist, alliterator and enunciator out there in hip-hop music. In terms of rapping about the pain that other disenfranchised people feel, there is no one better at their game than Eminem"
